My Dearest...

The Valentine Correspondence

I. The Universal Truth

It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a heart once touched by your grace is forever altered. In the quiet hours of reflection, I find myself marvelling at how one soul can so thoroughly occupy the thoughts of another. Just as your beloved Elizabeth Bennet possessed 'fine eyes' and a spirit that refused to be quelled, you have brought a light into my life that no other could hope to mimic.

II. The First Volume

I think often of the book that started it all for you. The pages that taught you of Darcy’s pride and Elizabeth’s prejudice. You read those words and saw a world of dignity and deep, hidden devotion—never knowing that one day, you would become the heroine of a story just as profound. Your mind is your greatest estate, more valuable than any manor in Derbyshire.

III. A Proposal of the Heart

I must confess, I have not the eloquence of the poets you admire, nor the grand declarations of the stage. But I offer you a heart that is yours entirely. To be your Valentine is not a request for a single day, but a petition to be the one who walks beside you through the gardens of Pemberley and the quiet rooms of home alike.

IV. The Unending Story

As we turn the pages together, I find that my favorite parts are always those where we are simply *us*. Your laughter is the music of my life, and your happiness is the only goal I find worth pursuing. Let us make this day the beginning of a new volume, one filled with the kind of love that Jane Austen herself would have been proud to pen.
